### Year: 1965
### Patient profile
male with type-2 diabetes and hypertension

### Subjective  (≈200-250 words)
The patient reports feeling fatigued and has been experiencing increased thirst and urination. He denies any chest pain, shortness of breath, or abdominal discomfort. His appetite has been stable, and he reports occasional dizziness when standing up quickly. The patient expresses concerns about managing his diabetes and hypertension effectively.

### Objective / Vitals  (≈200-250 words)
On examination, the patient appears well-nourished but overweight. Blood pressure is elevated at {{150/90}} mmHg, and heart rate is regular at {{80}} beats per minute. Respiratory rate is {{16}} breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation is {{98%}} on room air. No peripheral edema is noted. Blood glucose levels are elevated at {{200 mg/dL}}. 

### Labs & Imaging  (≈200-250 words)
Laboratory results reveal elevated HbA1c levels of {{8.5%}}, indicating poor long-term glucose control. Lipid panel shows elevated LDL cholesterol levels. Urinalysis demonstrates microalbuminuria, suggestive of early kidney involvement. Chest X-ray is unremarkable, showing clear lung fields and normal cardiac silhouette.

### Assessment  (≈200-250 words)
The patient is a middle-aged male with uncontrolled type-2 diabetes and hypertension. His symptoms of fatigue, polyuria, and polydipsia are consistent with poorly controlled diabetes. Elevated blood pressure and abnormal laboratory findings indicate increased cardiovascular risk. Early signs of kidney involvement are noted. The patient's weight and lifestyle factors contribute to his current health issues.

### Plan  (≈200-250 words)
The management plan includes optimizing the patient's diabetes and hypertension control through lifestyle modifications, medication adjustments, and regular monitoring. Initiating antihypertensive therapy and intensifying diabetes treatment are essential. Dietary counseling and exercise recommendations will be provided to promote weight loss and improve glycemic control. Regular follow-up visits for monitoring blood pressure, glucose levels, and kidney function are scheduled. Patient education on the importance of medication adherence, blood pressure control, and self-monitoring of glucose levels is crucial for long-term management.
