Additionally, he reports occasional episodes of nocturnal dyspnea. Overall, the patient expresses frustration with his declining respiratory function and its impact on his daily activities.

Auscultation reveals diffuse expiratory wheezes and decreased breath sounds at the lung bases. His oxygen saturation on room air is {{number}}%. The patient's blood pressure is within normal limits, and he has a regular heart rate without murmurs or gallops. 

### Labs & Imaging  (≈200-250 words)
Laboratory results show elevated inflammatory markers, including an increased C-reactive protein level. Arterial blood gas analysis reveals respiratory acidosis with hypoxemia. Chest X-ray demonstrates hyperinflation with flattened diaphragms, consistent with COPD. Pulmonary function tests confirm a severe obstructive pattern with reduced FEV1/FVC ratio.

### Assessment  (≈200-250 words)
The patient is a {{age}}-year-old male with a history of smoking and COPD presenting with worsening dyspnea, chronic cough, and wheezing. Physical examination and diagnostic tests are consistent with an exacerbation of his COPD. The patient's symptoms and objective findings indicate acute respiratory distress and impaired gas exchange, likely due to airway inflammation and bronchoconstriction.

### Plan  (≈200-250 words)
The plan for management includes initiating bronchodilator therapy with inhaled short-acting beta-agonists and anticholinergics to relieve bronchospasm. Systemic corticosteroids will be administered to reduce airway inflammation. Oxygen therapy will be provided to maintain adequate oxygenation. Close monitoring of the patient's respiratory status, oxygen saturation, and response to treatment is essential. Smoking cessation counseling and pulmonary rehabilitation will be recommended to optimize long-term lung function and quality of life. In case of treatment failure or worsening respiratory distress, consideration will be given to escalating therapy and possible hospitalization.
