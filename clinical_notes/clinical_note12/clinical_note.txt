The patient expresses frustration with his limited mobility and decreased quality of life due to his respiratory symptoms.

### Labs & Imaging  (≈200-250 words)
Laboratory findings reveal elevated C-reactive protein levels suggestive of an underlying inflammatory process. Arterial blood gas analysis shows respiratory acidosis with partial pressure of oxygen (PaO2) of {{60 mmHg}} and partial pressure of carbon dioxide (PaCO2) of {{55 mmHg}}. Pulmonary function tests demonstrate a severe obstructive pattern with reduced forced expiratory volume in one second (FEV1) to {{40%}} of predicted.

### Assessment  (≈200-250 words)
The patient is a {{55}}-year-old male with a history of smoking and COPD presenting with acute exacerbation of his respiratory symptoms. He exhibits signs of respiratory distress with hypoxemia and hypercapnia. The chest X-ray findings are consistent with his known diagnosis of COPD. The elevated inflammatory markers suggest an infectious exacerbation or exacerbation due to environmental triggers. Pulmonary function tests confirm severe airflow limitation consistent with advanced COPD.

### Plan  (≈200-250 words)
The patient will be started on supplemental oxygen therapy to maintain oxygen saturation above {{92%}}. Nebulized bronchodilators and systemic corticosteroids will be initiated to alleviate bronchoconstriction and reduce airway inflammation. Antibiotics will be considered if there is evidence of a bacterial infection. The patient will receive chest physiotherapy to aid in sputum clearance. Smoking cessation counseling will be provided, and the patient will be encouraged to continue pulmonary rehabilitation to improve his exercise tolerance and quality of life.
