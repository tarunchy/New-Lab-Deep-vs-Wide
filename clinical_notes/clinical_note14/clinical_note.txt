Patient profile: male former smoker with COPD

--- 1965 ---
### Year: 1965
### Patient profile
male former smoker with COPD

### Subjective (≤60 words)
Patient reports increased shortness of breath with minimal exertion and productive cough. Denies chest pain or fever.

### Objective / Vitals (≤80 words)
Vital signs stable. Respiratory rate slightly elevated at 22 breaths per minute. Decreased breath sounds and expiratory wheezing on lung auscultation.

### Labs & Imaging (≤80 words)
Chest X-ray shows hyperinflated lungs with flattened diaphragms. Arterial blood gas reveals hypoxemia and hypercapnia.

### Assessment (≤80 words)
Exacerbation of COPD with acute respiratory failure.

### Plan (≤80 words)
Initiate oxygen therapy, bronchodilators, and corticosteroids. Monitor closely for respiratory status and consider non-invasive ventilation if needed. Arrange follow-up with pulmonologist.

--- 1966 ---
### Year: 1966
### Patient profile
male former smoker with COPD

### Subjective (≤60 words)
Patient reports increased shortness of breath and wheezing, especially with exertion. Denies chest pain or coughing up blood.

### Objective / Vitals (≤80 words)
Patient appears dyspneic at rest. Vitals: HR 90 bpm, RR 22/min, SpO2 92% on room air. BP 145/95. Coarse breath sounds bilaterally with prolonged expiratory phase.

### Labs & Imaging (≤80 words)
CXR shows hyperinflation and flattened diaphragms consistent with COPD. ABG reveals respiratory acidosis. CBC and BMP within normal limits. Mild exertional dyspnea noted on exercise stress test.

### Assessment (≤80 words)
Exacerbation of COPD with acute respiratory failure. Co-morbid hypertension. Stable cardiac function. No evidence of infection.

### Plan (≤80 words)
Initiate oxygen therapy to maintain SpO2 > 90%. Nebulized bronchodilators and corticosteroids for acute exacerbation. Monitor ABG and adjust oxygen flow as needed. Educate on smoking cessation and pulmonary rehabilitation. Follow-up in 1 week for reassessment.
