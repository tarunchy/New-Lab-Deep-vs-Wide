--- 1965 ---
### Year: 1965
### Patient profile
male with type-2 diabetes and hypertension

### Subjective  (≈200-250 words)
The patient reports feeling fatigued and has been experiencing increased thirst and urination. He denies any chest pain, shortness of breath, or dizziness. His appetite has been poor, and he has unintentionally lost weight over the past few months. The patient also mentions occasional blurry vision and numbness in his feet. He expresses concerns about managing his diabetes and hypertension effectively.

### Objective / Vitals  (≈200-250 words)
On examination, the patient appears tired but is alert and oriented. His blood pressure is {{140/90}} mmHg, heart rate {{80}} bpm, respiratory rate {{16}} breaths per minute, and temperature {{98.6}}°F. His BMI is {{30}}, indicating obesity. His fundoscopic exam shows signs of diabetic retinopathy. Neurological examination reveals decreased sensation in both feet. 

### Labs & Imaging  (≈200-250 words)
Laboratory results show elevated fasting blood glucose levels of {{200 mg/dL}}, confirming poorly controlled diabetes. His HbA1c is {{9%}}, indicating long-term hyperglycemia. Lipid panel reveals elevated LDL cholesterol levels. Urinalysis demonstrates microalbuminuria. An ECG shows normal sinus rhythm with no acute changes. Chest X-ray is unremarkable.

### Assessment  (≈200-250 words)
The patient is a middle-aged male with poorly controlled type-2 diabetes and hypertension. He presents with symptoms of hyperglycemia, including fatigue, polyuria, polydipsia, unintentional weight loss, and visual disturbances. Physical exam findings are consistent with diabetic retinopathy and peripheral neuropathy. Laboratory results confirm uncontrolled diabetes with evidence of end-organ damage. His hypertension is also inadequately managed, putting him at increased risk for cardiovascular complications.

### Plan  (≈200-250 words)
The treatment plan includes optimizing the patient's diabetes management through lifestyle modifications, oral antidiabetic medications, and insulin therapy as needed. Blood pressure control will be achieved through medication adjustments, dietary changes, and regular monitoring. The patient will be educated on proper foot care to prevent complications of neuropathy. Ophthalmology referral is recommended for further evaluation and management of diabetic retinopathy. Lifestyle counseling on diet, exercise, and smoking cessation will be provided to reduce cardiovascular risk factors. Follow-up appointments will be scheduled to monitor the patient's progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

Imaging studies do not reveal any acute abnormalities. The patient's dyspnea may be attributed to his uncontrolled hypertension, as evidenced by his elevated blood pressure readings.

### Assessment  (≈200-250 words)
The patient is a middle-aged male with a history of type-2 diabetes and hypertension. His current symptoms of fatigue, polyuria, polydipsia, unintentional weight loss, and poor glycemic control are concerning for uncontrolled diabetes. The elevated blood pressure and occasional dyspnea suggest inadequate management of his hypertension. Further evaluation is needed to assess end-organ damage and cardiovascular risk associated with his poorly controlled chronic conditions.

### Plan  (≈200-250 words)
The treatment plan includes optimizing the patient's diabetes management through lifestyle modifications, medication adjustments, and regular monitoring of blood glucose levels. The patient will be educated on the importance of medication adherence, dietary changes, and regular follow-up visits to prevent complications associated with uncontrolled diabetes and hypertension.
