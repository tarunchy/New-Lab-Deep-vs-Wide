Patient profile: male former smoker with COPD

--- 1965 ---
### Year: 1965
### Patient profile
male former smoker with COPD

### Subjective (≤60 words)
Patient reports increased shortness of breath and coughing, especially with exertion. Denies chest pain or wheezing.

### Objective / Vitals (≤80 words)
Vitals: BP 140/90, HR 88, RR 24, SpO2 92% on room air. Lung exam reveals decreased breath sounds and diffuse wheezing.

### Labs & Imaging (≤80 words)
CXR shows hyperinflated lungs with flattened diaphragms. ABG reveals respiratory acidosis with pH 7.32, pCO2 55 mmHg, pO2 60 mmHg.

### Assessment (≤80 words)
Exacerbation of COPD with acute respiratory failure. Likely triggered by infection or environmental exposure. Patient at risk for respiratory decompensation.

### Plan (≤80 words)
Initiate oxygen therapy to maintain SpO2 >90%. Nebulized bronchodilators and systemic corticosteroids for acute exacerbation. Monitor closely for signs of respiratory distress and consider ICU admission if condition worsens. Arrange follow-up with pulmonologist for optimization of COPD management.
