--- 1965 ---
### Year: 1965
### Patient profile
male former smoker with COPD

### Subjective (≤60 words)
Patient reports increased shortness of breath with minimal exertion and productive cough. Denies chest pain or fever.

### Objective / Vitals (≤80 words)
Vital signs stable. Respiratory rate slightly elevated at 22 breaths per minute. Decreased breath sounds and expiratory wheezing on lung auscultation.

### Labs & Imaging (≤80 words)
Chest X-ray shows hyperinflation and flattened diaphragms consistent with COPD. Arterial blood gas reveals hypoxemia and hypercapnia.

### Assessment (≤80 words)
Exacerbation of COPD with acute respiratory failure.

### Plan (≤80 words)
Initiate oxygen therapy, bronchodilators, and corticosteroids. Monitor respiratory status closely and consider non-invasive ventilation if condition worsens. Arrange follow-up with pulmonologist.
